## Download and Installation {#download}

### Step 1: Download DaVinci Resolve

1. Go to **blackmagicdesign.com**
2. Click "Support" or navigate directly to the download page
3. Select **DaVinci Resolve** (not Studio - the free version is perfect for beginners)
4. Choose your operating system:
   - Windows (download .exe file)
   - Mac (download .dmg file)
   - Linux (download .zip file)
5. Click "Download"

The download is about 2-3 GB, so it may take 10-20 minutes depending on your internet speed.

### Step 2: Install DaVinci

**Windows:**
1. Double-click the .exe file
2. Follow the installation wizard
3. Choose "Install for all users" (recommended)
4. Click "Finish"
5. Restart your computer

**Mac:**
1. Double-click the .dmg file
2. Drag DaVinci icon to Applications folder
3. Wait for copy to complete
4. Open Applications folder
5. Double-click DaVinci Resolve
6. Approve any security warnings

**Linux:**
1. Extract the ZIP file
2. Open terminal in extracted folder
3. Run: `./install.sh`
4. Follow prompts

### Step 3: Launch DaVinci

Double-click the DaVinci Resolve icon. The first launch takes 30-60 seconds.

You'll see a welcome screen asking you to create an account (optional for free version) and choose your workspace.

**Select: "Edit" workspace** (best for beginners)

Congratulations. You're ready to edit.

## Understanding the Interface {#interface}

When DaVinci opens, you'll see several panels. Let's break down what each does.

### The 5 Main Panels

**1. Media Pool (Left Panel)**

This is where all your video clips, photos, and audio files live. Think of it like a filing cabinet.

- **Import media:** Drag and drop files here or click "Import Media"
- **Organize:** Create folders for organization
- **Preview:** Click any clip to preview it

**2. Timeline (Center Panel)**

The timeline is where the magic happens. This is where you arrange your clips in order, add effects, and control timing.

- **Video track (V1):** Your main video clips go here
- **Audio track (A1, A2):** Audio/music goes here
- **Playhead:** The vertical line shows what you're watching
- **Zoom:** Scroll to zoom in/out on timeline

**3. Viewer (Right Panel)**

A large preview of what you're watching. Play, pause, and scrub through video here.

- **Play button:** Press space bar or click to play
- **Timeline slider:** Drag to jump to any moment
- **Full screen:** Click to watch full screen

**4. Inspector (Right Side)**

Shows detailed properties of selected clips. For now, ignore this - you don't need it for basic editing.

**5. Toolbar (Top)**

Quick access buttons for common tasks.

## Your First Project - Step by Step {#first-project}

Let's create your first video project from scratch. We'll make a simple video with 3 clips and music.

### Step 1: Create a New Project (2 minutes)

1. Open DaVinci Resolve
2. Click "New Project"
3. Name it "My First Video"
4. Click "Create"

You're now in a blank project, ready to add content.

### Step 2: Import Your Media (3 minutes)

First, you need video clips. If you don't have any, use your phone to film 3 short 15-30 second videos.

**To import videos:**
1. In the **Media Pool** (left panel), click "Import Media"
2. Navigate to your videos
3. Select 2-3 video files
4. Click "Open"

You'll see your clips appear in the Media Pool. Great! You now have content to edit.

**Pro Tip:** You can also drag and drop files directly into the Media Pool.

### Step 3: Add Clips to Timeline (5 minutes)

Now let's arrange your clips in order.

1. **First clip:** Drag it from Media Pool to the timeline (V1 track)
2. You'll see the clip appear on the timeline
3. **Second clip:** Drag to the timeline after the first clip
4. **Third clip:** Drag after the second clip

Your three clips are now arranged in a sequence. You've created your first edit!

### Step 4: Preview Your Edit (2 minutes)

1. Move the playhead (vertical line) to the start of the timeline
2. Press the **Play button** (or press spacebar)
3. Watch your video play
4. Notice transitions between clips
5. Press spacebar to stop

Congratulations. You've created your first video sequence.

### Step 5: Add Music (5 minutes)

A video is much better with music. Let's add background music.

**Getting music:**
- Use free music: sites like Pixabay, YouTube Audio Library, Epidemic Sound
- Download an MP3 file to your computer

**Adding music:**
1. Import the music file (same as Step 2)
2. Drag the music file to the **A1 audio track**
3. Position it at the start of the timeline
4. Music will play underneath your video

### Step 6: Adjust Clip Length (5 minutes)

Your clips might be too long or too short. Let's trim them.

**To trim a clip:**
1. Hover over the edge of a clip on the timeline
2. Click and drag the edge left or right
3. The clip gets shorter or longer
4. Release to confirm

Now your clips are the right length.

### Step 7: Add Transition (3 minutes)

Transitions smooth the jump between clips. Let's add one.

**To add a transition:**
1. In toolbar, look for "Edit" tab
2. Click "Transitions"
3. Select a transition (try "Cross Dissolve" - it's simple and professional)
4. Drag the transition to the space between two clips
5. Preview the result

Your clips now have smooth transitions instead of hard cuts.

### Step 8: Export Your Video (5 minutes)

Time to save your finished video.

1. Go to **File > Export > Video File**
2. Choose format: **MP4** (most universal)
3. Choose resolution: **1920x1080** (Full HD is standard)
4. Choose a save location
5. Click "Export"

DaVinci will process your video (takes 1-5 minutes depending on length and complexity).

**Congratulations!** You've created your first video from start to finish.

## 10 Essential Concepts {#concepts}

### Concept 1: Resolution

**What is it?** The size/clarity of your video. Measured in pixels.

**Common resolutions:**
- 1920x1080 (Full HD) - Standard, use this
- 3840x2160 (4K) - Highest quality, requires powerful computer
- 1280x720 (HD) - Lower quality, use only for older videos

**Beginner tip:** Use 1920x1080. It's the standard for YouTube and most platforms.

### Concept 2: Frame Rate (FPS)

**What is it?** How many individual images per second. Higher = smoother.

**Common frame rates:**
- 24 fps - Cinematic look
- 30 fps - Standard for video
- 60 fps - Smooth (used for sports, gaming)

**Beginner tip:** Use 30 fps. It's standard and works for everything.

### Concept 3: Codec

**What is it?** The compression method used to make your video smaller.

**Common codecs:**
- H.264 - Universal, works everywhere
- H.265 - Newer, smaller files, not all devices support
- ProRes - Professional, massive files

**Beginner tip:** Use H.264. It's universal and works on all devices.

### Concept 4: Color Grading

**What is it?** Adjusting colors to match your style or mood.

**Basic color adjustments:**
- Brightness - Make image lighter/darker
- Saturation - Make colors more/less vibrant
- Hue - Shift colors (make warm/cool)

**Beginner tip:** Start with simple brightness/contrast adjustments. Master color grading later.

### Concept 5: Keyframes

**What is it?** Markers that tell DaVinci "at this moment, this value should be this number."

**Uses:**
- Animate text position
- Fade audio in/out
- Create motion effects

**Beginner tip:** You don't need keyframes for basic editing. Learn this when you're ready for animation.

### Concept 6: Tracks

**What is it?** Horizontal lines where clips sit. Each track is independent.

**Track types:**
- Video tracks (V1, V2, V3) - Video clips
- Audio tracks (A1, A2, A3) - Sound and music

**Beginner tip:** You typically need only 1 video track and 1-2 audio tracks. More tracks = more complex edits.

### Concept 7: Rendering vs. Playing

**Playing:** DaVinci tries to show you video in real-time

**Rendering:** DaVinci converts your project into a final video file

**When to know the difference:**
- Many effects = slow playback
- Effects render perfectly when exporting
- Rendering = waiting (can be 10 minutes to hours)

**Beginner tip:** Don't worry about rendering speed. Final quality is what matters.

### Concept 8: RAW vs. Compressed Video

**RAW:** Uncompressed video directly from camera - huge files, highest quality

**Compressed:** Edited video in H.264 or similar - small files, still high quality

**When to use:**
- RAW: Professional work where quality is critical
- Compressed: YouTube, social media, normal projects

**Beginner tip:** Use compressed H.264. It's perfect for 99% of projects.

### Concept 9: Preview vs. Proxy

**Preview:** Playing video at full resolution (can be slow)

**Proxy:** Lower quality version for faster playback (appears high quality when exporting)

**How to use:**
- If video stutters during playback: Generate proxies
- DaVinci > Preferences > Project Settings > Proxies
- Playback becomes smooth

**Beginner tip:** You don't need proxies starting out. Use them if playback gets slow.

### Concept 10: Undo and Undo History

**Undo:** Press Ctrl+Z (Windows) or Cmd+Z (Mac)

**Redo:** Press Ctrl+Y (Windows) or Cmd+Y (Mac)

**Undo History:**
- Edit > Undo History
- See your last 100+ changes
- Click any change to revert to that point

**Beginner tip:** Press Ctrl+Z constantly. Make mistakes, undo them, learn from them.

## Common Beginner Mistakes {#mistakes}

### Mistake #1: Trying to Learn Everything at Once

❌ **Bad:** Open DaVinci and try to master color grading, fusion, effects, etc.

✅ **Good:** Master basic editing (clip trimming, transitions) first. Learn effects later.

### Mistake #2: Not Using Transitions

❌ **Bad:** Hard cuts between every clip (looks amateur)

✅ **Good:** Simple transitions (cross dissolve) between clips (looks professional)

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Audio

❌ **Bad:** Video with just camera audio or no audio

✅ **Good:** Video with background music, sound effects, good audio levels

### Mistake #4: Over-Complicating Effects

❌ **Bad:** 10 effects on every clip

✅ **Good:** 1-2 intentional effects per clip

### Mistake #5: Wrong Resolution/Frame Rate

❌ **Bad:** Mixing 1080p and 4K footage in same project

✅ **Good:** Choose one resolution (1920x1080) and stick with it

### Mistake #6: Not Organizing Media

❌ **Bad:** 100 loose files in Media Pool with confusing names

✅ **Good:** Organized folders (Footage, Music, Graphics, etc.)

### Mistake #7: Exporting Wrong Format

❌ **Bad:** Exporting as ProRes or other rare formats

✅ **Good:** Exporting as MP4 H.264 (works everywhere)

### Mistake #8: Not Using Adjustment Clips

❌ **Bad:** Applying color correction to every clip individually

✅ **Good:** Use one adjustment clip to affect all clips below it

## FAQ {#faq}

**Q1: Is DaVinci Resolve really free?**
A: Yes. The free version is fully functional for editing, effects, and color grading. You only pay $295 for Studio if you need advanced features (most beginners never do).

**Q2: Can I edit on a Mac/Linux/Windows?**
A: Yes, DaVinci works on all three.

**Q3: How much computer power do I need?**
A: Minimum: 8GB RAM, modern processor. Recommended: 16GB RAM, SSD drive. The more powerful, the better, but DaVinci runs on modest hardware.

**Q4: How long does it take to learn DaVinci?**
A: Basic editing: 2-3 hours. Intermediate: 1-2 weeks. Professional level: months. You can create good videos after your first week.

**Q5: Should I use DaVinci or another editor (Premiere, Final Cut)?**
A: DaVinci is best for beginners: free, integrated color grading, simpler interface.

**Q6: Can I edit 4K video?**
A: Yes, free version supports 4K. But 4K files are huge and slow on budget computers. Start with 1080p.

**Q7: How do I save my project?**
A: File > Save Project. DaVinci automatically saves periodically. Always manually save with Ctrl+S (Windows) or Cmd+S (Mac).

**Q8: What if my video plays slow?**
A: Generate proxy files (lower quality playback). Or reduce effects temporarily during editing.
